Netaji Subhas Chandra Bose

An Indian Nationalist Leader And Freedom Fighter

Netaji Subhas Chandra Bose's Timeline

  • 1897 - Born on January 23 in Cuttack, Odisha, India. A significant year marking the birth of Netaji Subhas Chandra Bose, a prominent figure in India's struggle for independence.
  • 1919-1921 - Bose studied at Cambridge University in England. During this period, he was exposed to various political ideologies and began to develop a deep understanding of the global political landscape.
  • 1921 - He joined the Indian National Congress and became an active member of the party. Bose's entry into the political arena marked the beginning of his dedicated involvement in India's fight for freedom.
  • 1938 - Elected as the President of the Indian National Congress. His presidency reflected the widespread support and admiration he garnered within the political circles of the time.
  • 1939 - Resigned from the Congress leadership due to ideological differences. A critical turning point in Bose's political journey, as he chose to follow his principles even at the cost of stepping down from a prestigious position.
  • 1940 - Bose escaped house arrest and sought exile, eventually reaching Germany. Faced with adversity, Bose's resilience and determination led him to explore international avenues for support in India's quest for independence.
  • 1941 - Formed the Free India Center in Berlin and later the Indian Legion to support the Axis powers. Bose's strategic collaborations during this period showcased his efforts to garner global support for India's cause.
  • 1943 - "Netaji formed the Azad Hind Fauj (Indian National Army) in collaboration with the Japanese." The establishment of the Azad Hind Fauj marked a significant milestone, symbolizing Bose's commitment to creating a force dedicated to liberating India.
  • 1945 - "Bose's whereabouts became uncertain, and there are conflicting reports about his death in a plane crash in Taiwan." The mystery surrounding Bose's fate adds a layer of intrigue to his legacy, with varying accounts contributing to the enigma.
  • 1970 - "The Indian government officially acknowledged Netaji's death in the crash." The official recognition of Bose's death marked a pivotal moment in acknowledging his sacrifices and contributions to India's independence.
  • 1997 - "The Mukherjee Commission was set up to investigate Netaji's death, but its findings were inconclusive." Ongoing investigations and inquiries reflect the persistent efforts to uncover the truth about Bose's demise, with inconclusive outcomes leaving room for continued speculation.
    • Netaji Subhash Chandra Bose played a crucial role in India's struggle for independence and is remembered as a charismatic and dynamic leader.
